Demo 8 — Color-pattern diversity with and without history

EEB 187 · Wednesday May 20, 2026

TipThe thread from last week

Last week (Demo 7), Frédérich et al. 2026 told us that surgeonfish color patterns have weak phylogenetic signal (Acanthuridae K_mult = 0.11) and evolve fast within a constrained motif space. This week you’ll test the same question on your group’s family, but with your own pavo-derived continuous pattern measurements rather than the paper’s binary motif coding — and you’ll see what happens when you take measurement error seriously.

Frédérich, B., Mittelheiser, L., Gillet, A., Hodge, J. R., Laudet, V., & Dornburg, A. (2026). Rapid and repeated evolution of pigmentation patterns in reef fishes. BMC Biology, 24(46). DOI

Today’s three-part question

Are your group’s color-pattern variables (a) structured, (b) structured because of phylogeny, and (c) structured robustly enough to survive measurement noise?

Part What you do What you learn
1 — Standing diversity 20 species × ≥5 genera. Run pavo’s classify() + adjacent() per image. PCA + bivariate lm() tests across pattern statistics. What pattern axes vary across your family today — ignoring tree.
2 — Phylogenetic correction Same 20 species, same statistics. phyl.pca, PGLS, Blomberg’s K + Pagel’s λ on every pavo variable. Whether the structure you found in Part 1 is just shared ancestry, or signal that exists once you account for it.
3 — Measurement-error propagation One species, 5 images. Compute mean ± SD per pavo variable. Treat that SD as universal measurement error. Monte Carlo (200 iter) re-run of Parts 1 & 2. Which of your Part-1 / Part-2 results still hold once you admit your tip values were never point estimates.

The three parts build on each other: Part 2 corrects Part 1 for ancestry; Part 3 stress-tests both for noise.

Your bundle

You’ll work with the same family you analyzed in Demo 7. Your group code (from Demo 7) gets you into the FishView mini-bundle:

Group Family Mini-bundle code
1 Balistoidea (triggerfishes) trigger-2026
2 Pomacanthidae (angelfishes) angel-2026
3 Scarinae (parrotfishes) parrot-2026
4 Cirrhilabrini (fairy wrasses) fairy-2026
5 Acanthuridae (surgeonfishes) tang-2026
6 Hypsigenyinae (hogfishes) hog-2026
7 Halichoerine (Coris-allies wrasses) coris-2026

Your bundle contains 20 species total, spanning ≥5 genera, all matched to your group’s tree:

  • 19 of the 20 species ship one exemplar image each (images/<species>/exemplar.png).
  • 1 of the 20 species — the “error-species,” picked by the lecturer — ships a 5-image stack (images/<error_species>/img-1.png through img-5.png) instead of a single exemplar. Part 3 uses these five to estimate measurement noise.
  • <family>-mini-tree.tre — pruned tree with exactly those 20 tips.
  • species_traits.csv — four ecological traits from FishBase (trophic level, max depth, max length, vulnerability index) — used in §1.8 and §2.8.
  • README.txt — names the error-species and lists each shipped image.

For the walkthrough below, we use Chaetodontidae (butterflyfishes) as the shared example so we can all watch the same code produce the same plots in class.

R packages needed

  "pavo",       # color-pattern stats
  "ape",        # tree handling
  "phytools",   # phyl.pca, Blomberg's K
  "caper",      # PGLS
  "nlme",       # gls fallback for PGLS
  "plotrix",    # phytools dependency
  "tidyr",      # pivot_longer in Part 3 plots
  "ggplot2"
))

tidyr is new this week — Part 3 reshapes the 200 × 9 Monte Carlo result matrix for the diagnostic boxplots, and tidyr::pivot_longer is the cleanest way to do that.

What students turn in

  1. Part 1 figure — your family’s pavo PCA scatter (PC1 vs PC2) with species labelled.
  2. Part 2 comparison — PCA biplot, K + λ values for each pavo variable, one-sentence interpretation of which variables show signal.
  3. Part 3 figure — Monte Carlo CIs on K (or λ, or one PGLS slope) for your group’s data.
  4. One paragraph (≤250 words): “Does my family’s pattern diversity look phylogenetically structured? How much of that conclusion depends on the precision of pavo measurements? Compare to Frédérich’s surgeonfish result.”
